Hierarchical Relationship

Key Point: "Dementia (DE)" is an umbrella category indicating the patient has dementia. The specific etiology labels (AD, LBD, VD, FTD, etc.) indicate which type(s) of dementia. All patients with specific etiologies (e.g., AD=1) automatically have DE=1. Patients can have multiple concurrent etiologies (e.g., AD + VD for mixed dementia).

Example: A patient with Alzheimer's disease would have: DE=1, AD=1, and all other labels=0.

📊 Data Source

National Alzheimer's Coordinating Center (NACC) database - a comprehensive, longitudinal dataset from 39 Alzheimer's Disease Research Centers across the United States.

Feature Analysis: 187 Numerical Features

Feature Categories

  • Historical & Demographic (his_*): 27 features including age, sex, education, family history, lifestyle factors (smoking, alcohol), and medical history
  • Medication History (med_*): 23 features covering current medications for various conditions including antidepressants, antihypertensives, and dementia medications
  • Physical Examination (ph_*): 11 features including vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate), BMI, vision, and hearing assessments
  • Clinical Examination (exam_*): 64 features covering neurological examination findings including motor function, gait, reflexes, and cognitive symptoms
  • Cognitive Battery Tests (bat_*): 5 features from standardized cognitive assessments including MMSE (Mini-Mental State Examination) and MoCA (Montreal Cognitive Assessment)

Feature Properties

All 187 features are numerical, enabling direct processing by the transformer model without categorical encoding.
